Modular Bouldering Wall
Grapple with your life!

Our Product

Our latest product is the Modular Bouldering Wall. Bouldering is a popular pastime activity as well as a competitive sport which has grown in following over the last few decades. As a result, many indoor bouldering facilities have been built across the UK for local communities to enjoy. We decided that we can do better.

Personal

The Modular Bouldering wall brings the experience of bouldering to you. Our design is ideal for in-home use and can be placed free standing or on any flat surface. Stick our wall on your bedroom wall or play outdoors in your garden, we've given you autonomy.

Adaptable

Being able to modify the route using our system allows users to experience an infinite range of climbing problems. This will not only mean hours of fun, but it will improve your technique and ability to climb.

Sharing

Bouldering is a social sport and we have tried to capture that with our product. The wall is a modular design whereby multiple walls can be used together to increase the playing space. This means you’ll easily be able to climb with friends- it’s an adventure to share.

Economical

We know that bouldering as an activity is quite expensive. Equipment, travel and entry fees are not ideal, so we have scrapped all of it. A one time payment is all you need to become a part of the bouldering community.

Guide to registering

Step-by-step instructions to register and set up a domain name

  • Step 1

    Pick a domain name

    Create a unique domain name to use for your website. This will be used to access your site online so it must be different. Sites such as godaddy can be used to check if the domain name you want to choose is available.

  • Step 2

    Registering under a top-level domain

    The extension at the end of the domain (e.g “.com”, “.co.uk”, etc) is known as the top-level domain. Select one and register it using third party sites which allow you to purchase domains (different top-level domains have different prices and different access rights).

  • Step 3

    Purchasing your domain

    The domain you selected can be purchased in one-year increments up to 10 years. They can later be renewed for a fee if required.

  • Step 4

    Web-hosting

    Now that you have purchased your domain, your website should be live. However, the images, text and all related files need to be stored on a server so they can be accessed from anywhere. Choose a web-host (most domain registrars include some sort of web hosting in their services).

Tools to design a website

Using HTML and bootstrapping

What is Accessibility?

What considerations need to be made?

Accessibility in the context of web design refers to the inclusivity of a website to those who may be less able to use the functions of it. Visual, motor, auditory, cognitive or intellectual impairments can all prevent an individual from using the website with ease so accessibility aims to address the needs of those with such disabilities either through assistive technologies (speech recognition, braille terminals, screen magnification software, etc) or through the use of good web design (large icon features for those with less accurate motor skills, images associated with texts, closed captions, etc).

What is GDPR?

Data-handling Law

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is the standardised law across the EU nations with regard to data protection and privacy. It determines how personal information is displayed and stored across the internet and other forms of communication. The considerations taken to abide this regulation essentially involve gaining permission to use information and taking appropriate security precautions.

  • The request for consent must be given in an intelligible and easily accessible form, with the purpose for data processing attached to that consent. Consent must be clear and distinguishable from other matters and provided in an intelligible and easily accessible form, using clear and plain language. It must be as easy to withdraw consent as it is to give it.
  • GDPR introduces data portability – the right for a data subject to receive the personal data concerning them – which they have previously provided in a ‘commonly use and machine-readable format’ and have the right to transmit that data to another controller.
